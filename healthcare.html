Affordable Care Act

March 2010, President Obama signed the Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act (Obamacare) into law. This landmark legislation served as an history victory for all Americans across the country. The new law lowered health care costs and protects consumers from unfair insurance practices. The Congressional Budget Office estimates that it will reduce the national deficit by $210 billion. 

Since then, the Republican Congress has tried to repeal the Affordable Care Act over 36 times. Premiums are going up at the slowest rate in decades, but Republicans are more worried about cutting taxes for the wealthy and preserving loophols for insurers. They offered a plan, to cut off 25 million insured Americans and shrink Medicare and Medicaid for their most vulnerable neighbors. 1 million Pennsylvanians will no longer have health insurenace if the Republicans get their way.
